The reality on the floor

A retail owner today runs three shops stitched from four apps.

Every counter, every category, every compliance body wants something different. Nobody sells one system that fits all of it — so retailers end up with a POS that doesn't know their SKUs, a spreadsheet that doesn't know their bills, and a WhatsApp group where the receivables actually live.

Problem 01

Every category needs a different app

Jewelers need hallmark and daily rate. Pharmacies need batch and expiry. Grocers need scale. Buying six different softwares means six invoices, six vendors, six trainings, zero integration.

Problem 02

Data entry eats the day

Supplier bills come as photos, receipts, WhatsApp forwards. Someone retypes every line, every price, every HSN into an ancient POS. The owner reconciles at midnight — or doesn't.

Problem 03

Customization means months and lakhs

Ask a local vendor to "just add a batch column" and it's a three-month conversation ending in a new invoice. The floor gets no closer to the way the shop actually sells.

Weight, purity, hallmark — priced against today's rate.

Every piece is unique, every price is calculated live off gross and net weight, and every sale ends with a hallmark number on the invoice. Vistaar does all of it — no spreadsheet on the side.

The bill OCR isn't generic. It's shaped by the fields you told it to look for.

Capture

Photograph a bill. Get your fields back.

Handwritten, printed, four columns wide or twelve, the extraction reads only what your business tracks. A jeweler's bill returns gross weight and hallmark. A chemist's bill returns batch and expiry. Same engine, different vocabulary.

  • 55+ retail data points in the library — HSN, MRP, purity, batch, e-way bill, gold rate, making charge — each with a hand-tuned extraction instruction.
  • Fuzzy SKU match. "BENGAL HL BENARASI" and "Bengal H/L=UIV Benarasi" resolve to the same product. Approve, or create a new SKU inline.
  • Multi-page, handwritten, PDF or JPG. Any Indian bill format the AI has seen — and if it hasn't, one round of feedback teaches it.
  • Confidence flagged per line. Illegible items marked in yellow for review; the rest post straight to inventory.

Customization

Track anything the library doesn't. Zero code.

Something your trade uses that nobody else does? Add a field, write one line describing what it looks like on a bill, and the AI extracts it forever. What normally takes a three-month development cycle takes ninety seconds.

  • Per-surface selection. Turn a field on for supplier bills, sale bills, SKU form, customer record — independently.
  • Custom AI instructions. Write the extraction prompt the way you'd tell a new employee to look for it. The AI follows it verbatim.
  • Reorder, rename, require. Field position, label, required-or-optional — configured by the shop owner, no ticket to raise.
  • Same vocabulary everywhere. Enable a field once and it appears in the SKU form, on the invoice, and inside the OCR prompt — no drift.
